## 1. Mission statement

**Every mathematics teacher — regardless of school size, location, or language — should have a world-class, curriculum-aligned AI assistant.** MisMath AI exists to raise the quality and equity of math education in North Macedonia and the wider region by putting powerful, responsible AI tools directly into teachers' hands, in their own language.

## 2. The public-interest problem

- **Educational inequity.** Quality test-prep, adaptive practice, and rich instructional materials are concentrated in well-funded urban schools. Rural and under-resourced schools — and minority-language communities — lack them.
- **Teacher workload & burnout.** Hours spent on test-writing and planning reduce time for actual teaching and individual student support.
- **Minority-language exclusion.** Albanian- and Turkish-speaking students and teachers in North Macedonia are underserved by tools built only for majority languages or for Anglophone curricula.
- **AI-literacy gap.** As AI enters classrooms, teachers need structured training to use it **responsibly and effectively** — most have none.

## 3. How MisMath addresses it (public-interest value)

| Public-interest goal | How MisMath delivers |
|---|---|
| **Equity of access** | A low-cost (€25/yr) / free-tier AI assistant available to any teacher, anywhere with a browser — including offline-capable PWA |
| **Minority-language inclusion** | Full localization in **Macedonian, Albanian, Turkish, English** — instruction in the learner's/teacher's language |
| **Curriculum fidelity** | Mapped to the national MОН curriculum and БРО standards (grades 1–13 + vocational), so it strengthens — not bypasses — the public system |
| **Teacher AI literacy** | Built-in **professional-development academy** (a 23-chapter AI-literacy course aligned to the EU-OECD AI Literacy Framework) and a student **AI Code of Conduct** for responsible use |
| **Measurable learning outcomes** | Step-level cognitive telemetry, concept-mastery analytics, standards-coverage reporting — enabling evidence of impact |
| **Open educational resources** | A community **scenario bank** where teachers share curriculum-tagged lesson scenarios, multiplying public value |

## 7. Governance, transparency & reporting

- **Quarterly impact reports** with quantitative metrics (teachers reached, courses completed, resources shared, learning-outcome deltas).
- **Privacy-by-design:** telemetry is EU-hosted (PostHog), session recording/autocapture disabled, student data access hardened by role-based database rules (verified in the 23 Jul 2026 audit); a **Privacy Impact Assessment** already exists (`docs/MON_PRIVACY_IMPACT_ASSESSMENT.md`).
- **Open outputs:** curriculum-tagged scenarios and impact findings published as open educational resources where appropriate.
- **Independent evaluation** of the impact study.

## 8. Sustainability beyond the grant

Grant funding de-risks the **equity and impact layer** while the commercial model (Pro/School licenses, see `DONOR_FINANCIAL_PLAN.md`) sustains operations. The two are complementary: grant support expands access and evidence; commercial revenue sustains the platform. Domestic break-even is ≈36 paying Pro users, so the platform is not grant-dependent for survival.
